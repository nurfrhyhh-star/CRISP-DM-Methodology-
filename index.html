Introduction

CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) is a widely used data science methodology that provides a structured and iterative approach to solving real-world data problems.

Definition

CRISP-DM, which stands for Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining, is a widely used framework that defines the key stages of a data analytics or data science project. It provides a clear, structured approach that helps teams plan, manage, and execute projects effectively, ensuring that analytical work remains aligned with business goals. By following this methodology, organizations can improve efficiency, minimize costly mistakes, and increase the likelihood of delivering meaningful and actionable results from their data.

Origin

CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) was developed in the 1990s by a consortium of organizations including Teradata, SPSS, Daimler-Chrysler, and OHRA. It builds on earlier data analysis approaches such as KDD (Knowledge Discovery in Databases) and SEMMA, refining their ideas into a more practical and widely applicable framework for data science. The original goal of CRISP-DM was to provide a standardized way to structure data mining projects, making them faster, easier to manage, more repeatable, and effective across a wide range of industries.
