Problem Statement

Financial institutions face significant risk when borrowers fail to adhere to repayment schedules. This study applies the CRISP-DM methodology to predict loan default risks, transforming raw financial data into actionable lending intelligence.

Data Distribution

Non-Default (65%)
Defaulted (35%)

CRISP-DM Application Flow: Loan Default

Business Understanding

A commercial bank aims to reduce its 35% default rate by identifying high-risk applicants before loan approval.

Stage Output: Project plan and success metrics.
Data Understanding

Auditors examine historical logs of 50,000 past loans, looking at debt-to-income ratios and payment history.

Stage Output: Data quality report identifying missing credit scores.
Data Preparation

Cleaning the dataset by removing duplicate entries and normalizing salary figures into a standard format.

Stage Output: Integrated dataset ready for algorithms.
Modeling Phase

Applying classification models like Logistic Regression or Random Forest to predict "Default" vs "Non-Default."

Stage Output: Trained predictive algorithms.
Evaluation Phase

Testing the model on unseen data to see if it correctly caught the 35% of defaulted cases.

Stage Output: Accuracy and business alignment report.
Deployment Phase

Integrating the scoring model into the bank's digital portal for real-time risk assessment.

Final Output: Automated credit-risk scoring system that flags high-risk applicants.

Framework Assessment

AdvantagesLimitations
Structured Risk Management: Provides a systematic roadmap from defining business goals to model deployment. Time-Intensive: The iterative nature of Data Prep and Modeling can delay urgent credit policy updates.
Enhanced Decision Objectivity: Replaces subjective human bias with data-driven credit scores. Data Quality Sensitivity: If historical data contains missing credit scores, the model accuracy drops significantly.
Regulatory Transparency: Documentation at each stage helps banks explain "why" a loan was rejected to auditors. Algorithm Complexity: Advanced models (like Random Forest) can act as "black boxes."
